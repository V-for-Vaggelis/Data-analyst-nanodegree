Traffic patterns in the city center of Thessaloniki¶

by Athanasakis Evangelos¶

Investigation Overview¶

This investigation aims at identifying patterns in the traffic congestion in the city center of Thessaloniki. Some questions are addressed:

  1. What are the days and months of maximum and minimum congestion?
  2. What are the hours of maximum and minimum congestion?
  3. How does the hourly beghavior change by day?

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset used in this project, includes traffic data of 9 roads located in the city center of Thessaloniki from the years 2015 to 2020. These 9 particular roads, are located around the reference air-quality monitoring station which is the reason they are chosen. The data source is a project called TrafficThess for which you can find more information here. The data were accessed through the "historical data" option in this website. Originally the website provides one dataset for each road seperately, where the variables of interest are statistical parameters of the speeds of several taxis crossing the road and each observation differs by a 15 minute interval (or more) from the previous.The dataset provided here is an engineered version of all those datasets merged together. The average speed for each observation and the free flow speed for each road are accessed so that we can estimate a congestion index called tpi (traffic performance index). The data are also upsampled so that we have hourly observations. So we have as a result a dataset indexed by hourly timestamps and with 9 columns holding the congestion index for each road.

Daily average barplot¶

The average congestion for all days scales between 0.5 and 0.6 and the differnces between them are small. Weekends show smaller congestions and specifically Sunday seems to be the less congested day.

Monthly average barplot¶

No large differences are observed between months as well. Autumn and winter months (especially December) show larger congestions while summer months (especially August) show smaller congestions

Colormap of hourly average by day¶

It is clear that Tuesday and Thursday afternoons (around 19:00) are the most congested periods. Fridays seem to have the largest congestions at noon. All days seem to typically have one peak at noon (around 13:00) and one at afternoon (around 19:00). Finally around 05:00 is when the least congestion occurs.
